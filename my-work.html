I've done a number of things on the Internet, including written pieces, code stuff, etc.

Empire Minecraft

I am a player of a bit under 4 years on a wonderful Minecraft server called Empire Minecraft. I am a member of the Contribution Team of EMC, where I help keep the EMC Wiki up-to-date, relevant, and standardised. (An example of this standardisation is my spearheading of the update of all Custom Item renders from pre-1.14 textures to 1.16).
I also write blog posts for the EMC Blog, only one of which has been published: What’s New in the Minecraft 1.16 “Nether Update”.

Modrinth

I am an official Contributor to Modrinth, a FOSS modding platform similar to that of CurseForge, but far better. I do some level of quality assurance by ensuring issues with the software powering Modrinth are documented for the developers to remediate.
I've also submitted a couple minor changes to the website itself, which can be found here.

Mod Licensing Guide

Additionally, I've written a blog post for Modrinth's blog regarding software licensing for Minecraft mods. That guide can be found here.

Minecraft Mods

I've done a bit of work on helping others with Minecraft Fabric mod developing, as well as having made a couple of my own.
The ones listed below are simply my notable creations/contributions, and is not comprehensive of every contribution I've made to projects.

Pride Flags & Chocobacon

I've created two Fabric mods of my own: Pride Flags, and Chocobacon. The former introduces rainbow blocks and inventory holdables that look like pride flags representing various LGBTQ+ communities, and the latter introduces Chocolate Bars, Cooked Bacon, and Chocolate Bacon, which are unfortunately absent in the vanilla Minecraft food selection.

Consistency Plus

I am an official Code Contributor to Consistency Plus (thanks to this pull request), a Fabric mod dedicated to resolving disparities between consistency in the number of building blocks and materials provided to the Minecraft player.

Loading Timer

I am an official Contributor to Loading Timer (thanks to this pull request), a Fabric mod dedicated to giving the user a notification once the game has loaded displaying how much time the game took to load.
